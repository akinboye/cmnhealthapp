# Cure My Nation - Patient Health Information Guide

## Patient Rights

Every patient has fundamental rights that must be respected by healthcare providers:

1. **Right to Informed Consent**
   - You have the right to understand your medical condition, treatment options, and potential risks
   - You can ask questions before agreeing to any treatment
   - You can refuse treatment if you choose

2. **Right to Privacy and Confidentiality**
   - Your medical information is confidential
   - Healthcare providers cannot share your information without permission
   - You can request access to your medical records

3. **Right to Quality Care**
   - You deserve respectful, competent healthcare
   - You have the right to a second opinion
   - You can file complaints if you're not satisfied with care

4. **Right to Emergency Care**
   - Emergency services must be provided regardless of ability to pay
   - Life-threatening conditions take priority

## Common Health Conditions

### Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)
- Normal blood pressure: Less than 120/80 mmHg
- Elevated: 120-129/<80 mmHg
- Stage 1 Hypertension: 130-139/80-89 mmHg
- Stage 2 Hypertension: 140 or higher/90 or higher mmHg

**Management:**
- Regular monitoring
- Reduce sodium intake
- Exercise regularly
- Maintain healthy weight
- Manage stress

### Diabetes
- Type 1: Autoimmune condition where body can't produce insulin
- Type 2: Body can't effectively use insulin
- Gestational: Occurs during pregnancy

**Prevention:**
- Maintain healthy weight
- Eat balanced diet
- Exercise regularly
- Limit sugar and processed foods

### Mental Health
- Depression, anxiety, and stress are common health issues
- Mental health is as important as physical health
- Professional help is available and encouraged
- Various treatments including therapy and medication

## Healthcare Access

### Finding Healthcare
1. Government health centers provide basic services
2. Private hospitals offer specialized care
3. Pharmacies can provide over-the-counter medicines
4. Telemedicine options are increasingly available

### Vaccinations
- Essential for disease prevention
- Available at government health centers
- Most are free or low-cost
- Schedule according to WHO guidelines

### Maternal and Child Health
- Prenatal care is important for pregnant women
- Regular check-ups help identify complications early
- Immunizations protect children from preventable diseases
- Nutrition is critical during pregnancy and early childhood

## Healthy Lifestyle

### Nutrition
- Eat variety of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and proteins
- Limit salt, sugar, and saturated fats
- Stay hydrated - drink plenty of water
- Avoid excessive alcohol

### Physical Activity
- Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate exercise per week
- Include strength training 2-3 times per week
- Any activity is better than none
- Consult doctor before starting intense exercise

### Sleep and Stress
- Get 7-9 hours of quality sleep
- Maintain regular sleep schedule
- Manage stress through meditation, exercise, or hobbies
- Seek help if experiencing prolonged anxiety or depression

## Disease Prevention

### Infectious Diseases
- Wash hands regularly with soap and water
- Practice respiratory hygiene
- Use mosquito nets in malaria-prone areas
- Get vaccinated against preventable diseases

### Non-Communicable Diseases
- Regular health screenings
- Monitor blood pressure and cholesterol
- Maintain healthy weight
- Avoid tobacco and excessive alcohol

## Emergency Situations

### When to Seek Emergency Care
- Chest pain or pressure
- Difficulty breathing
- Sudden weakness or paralysis
- Severe allergic reactions
- Severe injuries or trauma
- Loss of consciousness

### First Aid Basics
- Call emergency services (in Nigeria: 112 or local ambulance number)
- Keep the person calm and comfortable
- Don't move a person with spine injury
- Apply pressure to wounds
- Stay with the person until help arrives

## Medication Safety

- Take medications as prescribed
- Store medications in cool, dry place
- Don't share medications with others
- Be aware of side effects and interactions
- Keep list of all medications you take
- Consult pharmacist or doctor before taking new medications

## Resources and Support

For health information and support in Nigeria:
- Contact your nearest health center
- Call health hotlines for advice
- Access online health resources
- Join support groups for chronic conditions
- Speak with healthcare providers about concerns

Remember: Prevention is always better than cure. Regular check-ups, healthy lifestyle choices, and early treatment of symptoms can prevent many health problems.
